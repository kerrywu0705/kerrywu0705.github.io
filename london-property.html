Why This Project?

As a foreign young professional working in London, I've had my fair share of struggles with renting. Every time I moved, the same questions came up: Am I overpaying? Why does rent vary so much within the same borough? If I save enough for a deposit, where should I buy?

Listing websites show you today's price, but they don't show you trends, don't help you compare across boroughs, and certainly don't tell you which areas might be undervalued.

So I decided to build it myself — using publicly available UK government data to create an analytical tool that tracks rents, compares property prices, and surfaces potential investment opportunities across London.

Key Findings

Prices Peaked, Rents Surged

Most boroughs saw property prices peak in 2022 then decline, while rents continued to climb relentlessly. Rental growth has outpaced price growth across the board since 2021.

£360K – £1.2M 2024 Median Price Range
£1,500 – £3,491 2024 Monthly Rent Range
+21% to +50% Rent Increase Since 2019

The Market Is Tilting Toward Buying

Under standard mortgage assumptions (4.5% rate, 75% LTV, 30-year term), the number of boroughs where monthly rent exceeds mortgage payments has surged dramatically.

1 → 12 Boroughs where rent > mortgage (2019 vs 2024)
124.8% Tower Hamlets rent-to-mortgage ratio
93.8% London-wide average ratio (2024)

Technical Overview

Data Sources

  • HM Land Registry — Price Paid Data
  • ONS — Private Rental Market Statistics

Pipeline

  • Python (Pandas, NumPy) for ETL
  • MySQL for data cleaning
  • Automated with single command

Analysis

  • 9-phase pipeline, 11 output CSVs
  • Custom investment scoring model
  • IQR outlier removal

Visualisation

  • 5 Tableau Public dashboards
  • Parameters, calculated fields
  • Cross-filtering, map layers
